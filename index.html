Phishing Awareness

Common attack vectors you should recognise.

🎭

Fake Login Pages

Attackers clone real banking or social-media login pages pixel-for-pixel, then harvest your credentials the moment you type them in.

📧

Suspicious Emails

Phishing emails spoof trusted senders, create urgency ("Your account will be closed!"), and embed links to malicious sites.

🎁

Fake Rewards & Prizes

"You've won an iPhone!" scams lure victims into submitting personal details or credit card numbers to claim a prize that never arrives.

🔗

Shortened URLs

Bit.ly-style links hide the true destination. Always expand a short URL before clicking, especially in unsolicited messages.

🧠

Social Engineering

Attackers impersonate colleagues, tech support, or executives to manipulate people into revealing passwords or transferring funds.

📱

Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Text messages pretending to be delivery companies or banks direct victims to fraudulent websites via embedded links.
